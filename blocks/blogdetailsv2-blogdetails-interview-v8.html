A Conversation with the Future of Design

We sat down with award-winning designer Maria Santos to discuss creativity, AI, and the evolving role of designers.

Maria Santos

Chief Design Officer at Nexus Labs

Former Apple, Google • 15+ years in design

Q: How has your approach to design evolved over the years?

When I started, design was about making things look good. Now it's about making things work well for people. The visual aspect is just one layer of a much deeper practice. We're solving human problems, not just aesthetic ones.

Q: What's your take on AI in design?

AI is a tool, like any other. It can generate options quickly, handle repetitive tasks, and even surprise us with unexpected solutions. But the judgment—knowing what's right for users, what aligns with brand values, what tells the right story—that's still deeply human.

Q: What advice would you give to aspiring designers?

Stay curious. The best designers I know are obsessed with understanding how things work—not just interfaces, but systems, behaviors, cultures. Read widely. Travel if you can. Talk to people different from you. Design is empathy made visible.

"Design is empathy made visible."

Q: What's next for you?

We're working on some exciting projects at Nexus Labs that I can't talk about yet. But I can say we're exploring how design can address some of the biggest challenges facing humanity—climate, health, education. Design has a role to play in all of it.

Interview conducted by James Wilson on December 20, 2025
