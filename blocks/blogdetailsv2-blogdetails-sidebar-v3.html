Mastering Color Theory in UI Design

A comprehensive guide to using color effectively in your digital products.

Color is one of the most powerful tools in a designer's arsenal. When used effectively, it can guide users, evoke emotions, and create memorable experiences. But mastering color theory takes practice and understanding.

Understanding the Color Wheel

The color wheel is the foundation of color theory. It shows the relationships between primary, secondary, and tertiary colors, helping designers create harmonious palettes.

Color Harmony Techniques

  • Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the wheel
  • Analogous: Colors adjacent to each other
  • Triadic: Three colors equally spaced on the wheel
  • Split-complementary: A color plus two adjacent to its complement

Accessibility Considerations

Always ensure sufficient contrast between text and background colors. WCAG guidelines recommend a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.
